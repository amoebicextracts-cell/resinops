# ResinOps private-beta terms and privacy notice — draft

This document is a product draft for review before publication. It is not a substitute for legal advice and does not satisfy the general-availability legal-review gate.

## Beta purpose

ResinOps is being provided to a limited group of invited cannabis operators for evaluation and workflow feedback. Beta features may change, be incomplete, or become temporarily unavailable. Operators should not use ResinOps as the only copy of operational, compliance, laboratory, inventory, employee, or financial records.

## Authorized use

Users must be authorized by the participating facility and must use their own account. They may not attempt to access another facility, bypass access controls, upload credentials into ordinary facility records, or use ResinOps for unlawful activity. Facility owners control membership and should assign the least-privileged role appropriate to each user.

## Safety and compliance

ResinOps does not replace approved SOPs, product labels, equipment manuals, laboratory results, licensed engineers or contractors, legal advice, or state and local requirements. AI output is decision support and must be independently verified before any safety-critical, pesticide, extraction, engineering, product-release, or compliance action.

METRC writes remain disabled during the private beta unless ResinOps completes vendor onboarding and the participating facility separately approves a controlled integration rollout.

## Data and retention

Participating facilities should upload only data necessary for the agreed beta workflows and should avoid unnecessary personal or sensitive information. ResinOps uses facility-scoped access controls and audit history, but no beta system can promise uninterrupted availability or zero risk.

Before leaving the beta, a facility may request an export and account closure through the designated support channel. The final published policy must state the actual retention period, backup-retention behavior, deletion timeline, subprocessors, contact method, and any legally required exceptions.

## Support and incidents

Operators should report problems through the designated beta support channel and include the displayed request reference, time, facility, and action attempted. They must never send passwords or API keys through support. Suspected cross-facility access, exposed credentials, destructive data loss, or unsafe integration behavior should be reported immediately.

## Feedback

Participants permit ResinOps to use beta feedback to improve the product. Feedback should not include confidential information that the participant is not authorized to share.
